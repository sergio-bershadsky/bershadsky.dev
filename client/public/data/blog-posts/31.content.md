## The Vision: Where Blockchain Meets Esports

Play2Live emerged in 2018 at the intersection of two explosive trends: blockchain technology and competitive esports streaming. The platform aimed to revolutionize how gamers, streamers, and viewers interact by introducing cryptocurrency-based incentives and tokenized engagement mechanics.

As Principal Engineer and Consultant, I managed 5 teams across 30+ people, overseeing AI, DevOps, Blockchain, Auth, and Backoffice teams. My key technical responsibility was the ETH-Tendermint bridge implementation—connecting Ethereum smart contracts with the Tendermint consensus engine for high-throughput blockchain operations.

The company raised $30M through their ICO and was recognized by EU-Startups as one of "Europe's most promising eSports startups" and among "10 Maltese startups to watch in 2019."

## The Challenge: Real-time at Scale

Live streaming platforms face unique engineering challenges. Every interaction needs to happen instantly: chat messages, notifications, donations, and engagement features. Add blockchain wallet integration on top, and the complexity multiplies.

The platform needed to bridge traditional web authentication with blockchain wallet operations, support multiple social login providers, and handle the unpredictable traffic spikes that come with live esports events.

## Building the Foundation

### Unified Authentication

Gaming audiences expect seamless login experiences. We built an authentication system supporting five major social providers: Google, Facebook, Twitch, Steam, and VK. The challenge wasn't just connecting to these providers; it was handling the edge cases when users signed up through different methods but needed their accounts merged.

We designed an intelligent account merging system that detected when the same person had multiple accounts and guided them through a secure verification process to consolidate their data and wallet associations.

### Real-time Infrastructure

For a streaming platform, latency is everything. We built the messaging backbone to handle thousands of concurrent connections with sub-second delivery times. The architecture used event-driven patterns that could scale horizontally as audience sizes grew during popular tournaments.

### Blockchain Integration

The platform's unique value proposition was its cryptocurrency integration. Users could earn and spend tokens through platform engagement. This required careful security architecture: wallet operations had to be both fast enough for real-time interactions and secure enough for financial transactions.

## Engineering Culture

Leading a team in a fast-moving startup environment required balancing speed with quality. We established coding standards, automated testing practices, and deployment pipelines that let us ship features quickly while maintaining system stability.

The cross-functional collaboration with DevOps and Product teams was essential. Daily standups, weekly planning sessions, and open communication channels kept everyone aligned as requirements evolved with market feedback.
